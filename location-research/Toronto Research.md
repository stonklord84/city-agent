## **North York** 

## **Neighborhood Name & Boundaries** 

- North-central district of Toronto. 

- Roughly bordered by Steeles Avenue to the north, Eglinton Avenue to the south, Victoria Park Avenue to the east, and the Humber River/Jane Street area to the west. 

## **Vibe Description** 

- A diverse urban-suburban district that combines high-rise transit corridors with quieter residential neighborhoods. 

- Popular among newcomers, families, students, and professionals due to strong transit access, shopping, and relatively balanced housing options compared to downtown. 

## **Vibe Tags** 

- Diverse 

- Family-friendly 

- Professional 

- Transit-friendly 

- Multicultural 

- Suburban-urban mix 

## **Best Suited For** 

- Newcomers 

- Families 

- Young professionals 

- Students 

- Remote workers 

## **Safety Perception** 

- **8/10** 

- Generally regarded as a safe area with many established residential communities. Some pockets are busier than others, but North York is widely considered one of the more comfortable areas for families and newcomers. 

## **Walkability** 

- **7/10** 

- Highly walkable near Yonge Street, North York Centre, Finch, and Sheppard. More car-oriented in residential sections farther from major transit corridors. 

## **Transit Access** 

## ● **9/10** 

- Served by TTC Line 1 (Yonge–University) and Line 4 (Sheppard), with extensive TTC bus coverage throughout the district. 

- One of the best-connected areas outside Downtown Toronto for car-free living. 

## **Insider Tip** 

- If commuting regularly to downtown Toronto, living near Finch Station, North York Centre, or Sheppard–Yonge Station can significantly reduce travel times while remaining more affordable than downtown neighborhoods

## **Downtown Toronto** 

## **Neighborhood Name & Boundaries** 

- The central core of Toronto, generally bounded by Lake Ontario to the south, the Don River to the east, Bathurst Street to the west, and Bloor Street to the north. 

- Includes areas such as the Financial District, Entertainment District, CityPlace, St. Lawrence, Harbourfront, and parts of the Waterfront. 

## **Vibe Description** 

- Toronto's busiest and most urban area, with dense high-rise development, major employers, nightlife, restaurants, and cultural attractions. 

- Fast-paced and highly walkable, attracting young professionals, students, and people who prioritize convenience and city life. 

## **Vibe Tags** 

- Urban 

- Buzzy 

- Walkable 

- Professional 

- Nightlife 

- Dense 

## **Best Suited For** 

- Young professionals 

- Students 

- Remote workers 

- People without cars 

- Those who enjoy nightlife and city living 

## **Safety Perception** 

- **7/10** 

- Generally safe, but due to high population density there is more visible homelessness, petty crime, and late-night activity than in many suburban neighborhoods. 

## **Walkability** 

- **10/10** 

- One of the most walkable areas in Canada. Most daily needs can be reached on foot. 

## **Transit Access** 

- **10/10** 

- Direct access to TTC Lines 1 and 2, extensive streetcar routes, GO Transit, UP Express, and regional transit connections. 

- Living without a car is easy and common. 

## **Insider Tip** 

- Living a few blocks away from the busiest entertainment areas (King West, Queen West, Yonge-Dundas) can significantly reduce noise while keeping the same transit and amenity access.

## **OHIP (Ontario Health Insurance Plan)** 

## **What is it?** 

- Ontario's public health insurance program. 

- Covers doctor visits, hospital care, and many medically necessary services. 

## **How to apply** 

1. Go to a ServiceOntario centre in person. 

2. Bring: 

   - Proof of immigration status 

   - Proof of Ontario residence 

   - Personal identification 

3. Complete the application. 

## **After applying** 

- You receive a temporary confirmation with your health number. 

- Your physical OHIP card arrives by mail in roughly 2–4 weeks. 

## **Important** 

- There is currently no waiting period for eligible applicants. 

- Coverage begins when your application is approved. 

- Apply as soon as possible after arriving in Ontario. 

## **Help** 

- ServiceOntario INFOline: 1-866-532-3161
